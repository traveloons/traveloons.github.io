Vasudaiva Kutumbakam is a phrase from the Indian Philosophical text, the Maha Upanishad. The close-to-literal English translation of this would be : ‚ÄúThe world is just one family‚Äù.

While the phrase itself has meaning and context within the spiritual, historical and ethical realm, we have used it here in a very different sense. In fact we might have expanded on it to mean more than what it intended originally.

If one reads the phrase within Indian Philosophical context ‚Äì both the original text as well as the commentaries ‚Äì the notion of this world-family is mostly anthropocentric. The phrase is also quite metaphysical and less about the material realities.

We on the other hand used the phrase to include some real-world, pragmatic ideas such as removal of borders, dissolution of states and expanded on the family to include all life and non life in nature. For us the phrase includes the practicalities about being a family, about sharing, about equity and about love.

We consider calling it a website very reductive. In person, books, journals, magazines, study-circles there are many other ways we humans share ideas. So we chose to not call it by such names.

Moreover, blog was something we did not want to call it. Our content is not random musings or disconnected thoughts. Nor do we promise content with regularity. The modern form of the diary while did bring us together in some ways, also led to a whole lot of issues and expectations on the Internet; things we don‚Äôt believe in or cater to.
